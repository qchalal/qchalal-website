The Two Pathways to Indonesian Certification

Option 1: Foreign Halal Certificate Registration (MRA Path)

A streamlined process for products already certified by a foreign Halal Certification Body (HCB) that has a Mutual Recognition Agreement (MRA) with Indonesia’s BPJPH.

  • The Process: Instead of a full new audit, your existing certificate is registered through the BPJPH system.
  • The Difficulty: Only available if your local certifier is officially recognized by BPJPH.

Option 2: Full Regular Certification (The Standard Path)

Mandatory for domestic production or imported products if the certifier lacks an MRA with BPJPH.

  • The Process: Submission via the SIHALAL portal, an on-site audit by an authorized LPH (Halal Inspection Agency), and a fatwa issuance by the Indonesian Ulema Council (MUI).
  • The Difficulty: Highly technical, requiring deep-dive inspections, full raw material traceability, and proof of a robust Halal Product Assurance System (SJPH).

Why This Is Challenging

Obtaining certification in Indonesia is widely considered complex due to:

  • Regulatory Rigor: Requires continuous compliance and documentation across your entire supply chain.
  • System Complexity: Precise requirements in the SIHALAL portal often lead to rejections from minor input errors.
  • Category Mismatch: Choosing an LPH that lacks specific expertise in your industry can result in months of delays.
